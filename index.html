What is Pneumonia?

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus (purulent material), causing cough with phlegm or pus, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing. A variety of organisms, including bacteria, viruses and fungi, can cause pneumonia.

Risk Factors

  • Weakened immune system
  • Chronic lung diseases
  • Smoking or exposure to secondhand smoke
  • Age (young children and older adults are at higher risk)
  • Recent viral infections (e.g., flu or cold)
  • Hospitalization (especially in intensive care units)
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Malnutrition or poor overall health
  • Exposure to air pollution or toxic fumes

Prevention

  • Get vaccinated
  • Practice good hygiene
  • Avoid smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke
  • Strengthen your immune system
  • Avoid close contact with people who are sick
  • Cover your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing
  • Manage chronic conditions
  • Avoid excessive alcohol consumption
  • Keep your living environment clean and free of pollutants

Warning Signs

  • Persistent cough
  • Fever, sweating, and chills
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Chest pain when breathing or coughing
  • Fatigue and extreme tiredness
  • Loss of appetite
  • Confusion (especially in older adults)
  • Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
  • Bluish lips or nails
  • Rapid heartbeat or breathing
